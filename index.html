Herba Mystica

What is Herba Mystica?

Herba Mystica is a type of rare ingredient that can be added to a sandwich while picnicing in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet. What makes these ingredients so rare is that they can only be obtained by getting them as rewards for completing 5, 6, and 7 star Tera Raid Battles.

Additionally, you are not guaranteed an Herba Mystica as a reward for completing a raid and the drop rate is pretty low. The following guide will teach you how to farm Herba Mystica most efficiently.

Sandwiches

Sandwiches are craftable items in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet that, upon consumption, grant the player "Meal Powers" to increase the likelyhood of types of encounters when roaming Paldea.

There are currently 9 possible Meal Powers to obtain:

  • Catching Power:

    Increases the catch rate in battle

  • Encounter Power:

    Increases the spawn rate for a type of wild pokémon

  • Exp. Point Power:

    Increases the experience recieved for defeating a type of wild Pokémon

  • Raid Power:

    Increases the drop rewards from Tera Raids

  • Humungo Power:

    Increases the chance of a large Pokémon spawning

  • Teensy Power:

    Increases the chance of a small Pokémon spawning

  • Sparkling Power:

    Increases the chance of a shiny pokémon spawning

  • Title Power:

    Increases the chance of a wild Pokémon having a mark

  • Egg Power:

    Increases spawn rate of Pokémon Eggs while picnicing and increases hatch rate for eggs outside of picnics

Meal powers come in three levels, each level increase also increases the likelihood that you will find that Power Type.

For example - Raid Power:

  • Level 1: one extra item drop
  • Level 2: two extra item drop
  • level 3: three extra item drop

The only way to get your sandwich from level 2 to level 3 is by using Herba Mysticas. It may not seem like the effort is worth it at first, but a level 3 sandwich can make a tremendous difference if you're looking to complete your dex or hunt shiny pokémon.

Ingredients

Sandwich making can be a frustrating, trial and error process. Luckily some talented coders and patient memebers of the community have made it easy for you. You can find tons of helpful guides about what types of ingredients to use to maximize your output with less ingredients.

Checkout this guide (by twitter user @tak2525tak, translated by @SilentDestroySR) for a great example on how to build a level 3 shiny sandwich for each pokémon type.

Otherwise you can save yourself some time and ingredients with this sandwich making simulator (by github creator cecilbowen) and test your sandwich output before you make it.
